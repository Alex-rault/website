Convection Currents

Deep within the Earth, there are convection currents occur due to hot magma plums that rises towards the surface and then sinks back to the core as it gets cooler.

This motion then forces the techtonic plates at the surface to either converge, diverge or slide past each other.

As the oceanic plate goes deeper, it melts and new magma is formed and rises to the surface. The zone at the red dot is called the Benioff Zone- it is where earthquakes occur due to friction between the plates.
